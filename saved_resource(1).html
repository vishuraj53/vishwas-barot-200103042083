NEW DELHI/MUMBAI: In a fresh jolt to the Uddhav Thackeray-led Shiv Sena, Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla has recognised Rahul Shewale of the Sena faction led by Maharashtra chief minister Eknath Shinde as leader of the party in Lok Sabha. Acting on a representation signed by 12 of the 19 Sena members in the LS, Birla also recognised Bhawna Gawli as the party’s chief whip.

NEW DELHI: The Centre told the Lok Sabha on Tuesday that it does not subscribe to views of "foreign non-government organisation" Reporters Without Borders whose World Press Freedom Index has ranked India in the bottom 30 worst nations in a list of 180..

NEW DELHI: With 12 of 19 Shiv Sena Lok Sabha MPs attending a virtual meeting with CM Eknath Shinde on Monday, sources in the latter's camp said the members were set to form a separate group in the House.

MUMBAI: Responding to Uddhav Thackeray's statement that rebel Shiv Sena MLAs should face elections, CM Eknath Shinde said on Friday, without naming Thackeray, that he would quit politics if even a single one of 40 Sena MLAs who had backed him lost the next elections. Uddhav had remarked earlier that the rebels would lose if they contested polls. Shinde said, "It is said that none of the rebel MLAs will be elected. But I say no MLA will lose. I have taken responsibility for this. If any of these men lose, I will leave politics." He added, "Also, who are you to decide who will win and who will lose? All this is decided by people. Voters decide."

The war in Ukraine is a curious mix of old and new. Soldiers crouch in trenches that would not be out of place in Verdun, were it not for the glimpse of a reconnaissance drone above. Some Ukrainian gunners receive orders via Elon Musk’s Starlink constellation of satellites. Others fire artillery pieces that pre-date the Cuban missile crisis. Chinese-made quadcopters drop 1940s-vintage grenades on unsuspecting Russian tanks. It has the feel of a steampunk novel by Tom Clancy. Making sense of all this can be tricky. Conscription has ebbed away in America and most big European countries, so military matters seem rarefied. The Western wars of the past 30 years have been waged largely against assorted insurgents and guerrillas; the sound of big guns once more pounding out duels within Europe is disorienting.
